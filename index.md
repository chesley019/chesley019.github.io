## Qi Chen (陈琦)

<br>I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in [Prof. Endao Han's group](https://endaohan1988.wixsite.com/hanlab) at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore. Having worked with several research groups globally, my research interests encompass heat transfer, high-pressure gas jets, microfluidics, microscopic biofluid dynamics, and soft active matter. My current research focuses on the collective motion of active matter, using Myxococcus xanthus as a model bacterium.

<br>I obtained my Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees from Shandong University (SDU), China, where I worked with [Prof. Xuefang Li](https://faculty.sdu.edu.cn/lixuefang/en/index.htm) on the jetting dynamics of high-pressure hydrogen from nozzles. During this period, I also developed microfluidic setups to investigate the formation and behavior of monodisperse droplets and bubbles.

<br>I earned my Ph.D. from the School of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Manchester, UK, under the supervision of [Prof. Anne Juel](https://research.manchester.ac.uk/en/persons/anne.juel/) and [Dr. Igor L. Chernyavsky](https://math-biophys.info/wiki/start). For my doctoral research, I developed a biomimetic suspension model for red blood cells (RBCs) using monodisperse, ultra-soft PDMS capsules, which quantitatively mimics the large deformations of RBCs in confined tubes. Building on this framework, I investigated the rheological and dispersion behaviors of these soft suspensions within both ordered and disordered planar porous media.

<br>I am always open to academic discussions and potential collaborations. Please feel free to reach out to me at **qi_chen@ntu.edu.sg**.
